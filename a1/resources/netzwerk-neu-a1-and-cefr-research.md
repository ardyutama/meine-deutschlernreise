# Netzwerk Neu A1 & CEFR A1 Research

## 1. Netzwerk Neu A1 Structure

### Overview
- **Publisher:** Ernst Klett Sprachen GmbH (2019)
- **Authors:** Stefanie Dengler, Tanja Mayr-Sieber, Paul Rusch, Helen Schmitz
- **Total Chapters (Kapitel):** 12
- **Available formats:**
  - Complete volume: Kursbuch A1 (all 12 chapters)
  - Split editions: A1.1 (Kapitel 1–6) and A1.2 (Kapitel 7–12)
- **Components:**
  - Kursbuch (Textbook) – with integrated audio/video via app or online
  - Arbeitsbuch (Workbook) – practice exercises for each chapter
  - Intensivtrainer – additional practice
  - Testheft – test preparation booklet
  - Lehrerhandbuch (Teacher's manual)
  - Online platform (Klett Augmented / allango)
- **Exam preparation:** Goethe-Zertifikat A1: Start Deutsch 1, ÖSD Zertifikat A1

### Chapter Structure (each chapter)
Each Kapitel typically contains:
- **Einstieg** (Entry/warm-up) – introduction to the topic
- **3–4 learning sections** (A, B, C) with communicative tasks
- **Grammar boxes** (Grammatik) – highlighted grammar points
- **Pronunciation training** (Aussprache)
- **Mini-project or task** at chapter end
- **Culture/Landeskunde** section (DACH – Deutschland, Österreich, Schweiz)

After every 3 chapters: **Plattform** section with:
- Review activities
- Exam preparation tasks (Prüfungsvorbereitung)
- Video activities
- Project work

## 2. Netzwerk Neu A1 – All 12 Chapters

### Kapitel 1: Guten Tag!
- **Topics:** Greetings, introductions, saying goodbye, alphabet, numbers 0–20
- **Communication:** Greeting people, introducing yourself, spelling your name
- **Grammar:**
  - Personal pronouns (ich, du, Sie)
  - Verbs: sein, heißen (present tense, singular)
  - W-questions (Wie? Woher? Wer?)
  - Statement word order

### Kapitel 2: Freunde, Kollegen und ich
- **Topics:** Friends, colleagues, countries, languages, personal information
- **Communication:** Talking about yourself and others, saying where you're from, languages you speak
- **Grammar:**
  - Regular verb conjugation present tense (kommen, wohnen, sprechen)
  - Negation with nicht
  - Yes/No questions (Ja/Nein-Fragen)
  - Definite articles (der, die, das) – introduction

### Kapitel 3: In Hamburg
- **Topics:** City, places, transportation, directions (basic)
- **Communication:** Describing a city, asking where things are, numbers 20–100
- **Grammar:**
  - Indefinite articles (ein, eine)
  - Negation with kein/keine
  - Plural forms (introduction)
  - Prepositions with location (basic: in, auf)

### --- Plattform 1 (after Kapitel 3) ---

### Kapitel 4: Guten Appetit!
- **Topics:** Food, drinks, ordering in a restaurant/café, shopping for food
- **Communication:** Ordering food/drinks, expressing likes/dislikes, shopping dialogues
- **Grammar:**
  - Accusative case (den, einen) – direct objects
  - Verbs: mögen, möchten
  - Ja/Nein/Doch responses
  - Compound nouns

### Kapitel 5: Tag für Tag
- **Topics:** Daily routine, time, days of the week, appointments
- **Communication:** Telling time, describing your daily routine, making appointments
- **Grammar:**
  - Separable verbs (aufstehen, anfangen, einkaufen)
  - Time expressions (um, von...bis, am)
  - Sentence bracket (Satzklammer)
  - Temporal prepositions

### Kapitel 6: Zeit mit Freunden
- **Topics:** Free time, hobbies, weather, months, seasons
- **Communication:** Talking about hobbies, making suggestions, accepting/declining invitations
- **Grammar:**
  - Modal verb: können
  - Sentence bracket with modal verbs
  - Personal pronouns in accusative (mich, dich, ihn, sie, es)
  - Conjunctions: und, oder, aber

### --- Plattform 2 (after Kapitel 6) ---

### Kapitel 7: Willkommen in Berlin
- **Topics:** Berlin, tourism, sightseeing, travel
- **Communication:** Describing a trip, asking for directions, buying tickets
- **Grammar:**
  - Prepositions with dative (mit, zu, nach, von, aus, bei)
  - The verb "fahren" and other movement verbs
  - Ordinal numbers (first, second...)
  - Imperative (Sie-form)

### Kapitel 8: Beruf und Arbeit
- **Topics:** Professions, workplace, job descriptions, work activities
- **Communication:** Talking about jobs, describing what you do at work, work schedules
- **Grammar:**
  - Possessive articles (mein, dein, sein, ihr, unser, euer, Ihr)
  - Modal verbs: müssen, wollen
  - Temporal prepositions (seit, vor, in)
  - Word formation: profession names (-er/-erin, -in)

### Kapitel 9: Meine Wohnung
- **Topics:** Housing, rooms, furniture, living situations
- **Communication:** Describing your apartment/house, furniture, saying where things are
- **Grammar:**
  - Two-way prepositions (Wechselpräpositionen: in, an, auf, über, unter, neben, zwischen, vor, hinter) – with dative for location
  - es gibt + accusative
  - Adjectives (predicative, not declined)
  - Numbers 100–1.000.000

### --- Plattform 3 (after Kapitel 9) ---

### Kapitel 10: Alles Gute!
- **Topics:** Celebrations, birthdays, holidays, invitations, gifts
- **Communication:** Congratulating, inviting people, describing celebrations, dates
- **Grammar:**
  - Dative case with indirect objects (personal pronouns: mir, dir, ihm, ihr)
  - Verbs with dative (gefallen, gehören, helfen, schmecken)
  - Date expressions (am + ordinal number)
  - Conjunction: denn

### Kapitel 11: Gesund und fit
- **Topics:** Body parts, health, doctor visits, fitness, sports
- **Communication:** Describing health problems, at the doctor, giving advice
- **Grammar:**
  - Imperative (du-form, ihr-form)
  - Modal verb: sollen (for advice)
  - Reflexive pronouns (basic: sich fühlen)
  - Conjunction: weil (basic subordinate clause)

### Kapitel 12: Ab in den Urlaub!
- **Topics:** Vacation, travel planning, weather, past experiences
- **Communication:** Talking about vacations, describing past events, travel plans
- **Grammar:**
  - Perfekt (present perfect tense) – with haben and sein
  - Past participles (regular and irregular)
  - Sentence bracket with Perfekt
  - Time expressions for past (gestern, letzte Woche, vor einem Monat)

## 3. CEFR A1 Level Requirements

### Can-Do Statements (from Council of Europe)
**Overall:** "Can understand and use familiar everyday expressions and very basic phrases aimed at the satisfaction of needs of a concrete type. Can introduce themselves to others and can ask and answer questions about personal details such as where they live, people they know and things they have. Can interact in a simple way provided the other person talks slowly and clearly and is prepared to help."

#### Listening (Hören)
- Can understand simple instructions addressed carefully and slowly
- Can understand very basic phrases about personal details and immediate needs
- Can follow speech that is very slow and carefully articulated

#### Reading (Lesen)
- Can understand very short, simple texts (postcards, signs, forms)
- Can recognize familiar names, words and very basic phrases
- Can understand simple forms well enough to give basic personal details

#### Speaking – Interaction (Sprechen – Interaktion)
- Can interact in a simple way if the other person repeats or rephrases
- Can ask and answer simple questions about familiar topics
- Can use simple everyday polite greetings and phrases

#### Speaking – Production (Sprechen – Produktion)
- Can use simple phrases and sentences to describe where they live
- Can describe people they know using simple language
- Can introduce themselves with rehearsed phrases

#### Writing (Schreiben)
- Can write a short simple postcard
- Can fill in forms with personal details (name, address, nationality)
- Can write simple isolated phrases and sentences

### Vocabulary Size
- **Expected range:** 500–1,000 active words
- **Netzwerk Neu A1 word list:** approximately 800–1,000 words
- **Profile Deutsch A1:** ~600 productive words, ~1,300 receptive words
- Focus on high-frequency, everyday vocabulary

### Grammar Topics Covered at A1
1. Present tense (regular and irregular verbs)
2. sein, haben
3. Modal verbs (können, möchten, müssen, wollen, sollen)
4. Separable verbs
5. Imperative (basic forms)
6. Perfekt (present perfect) – introduction
7. Nominative, Accusative, and Dative cases (basic)
8. Definite and indefinite articles
9. Negation (nicht, kein)
10. Personal pronouns (nominative, accusative, dative)
11. Possessive articles
12. Basic prepositions (mit, in, nach, zu, von, aus, bei, um, für)
13. W-questions and Yes/No questions
14. Word order (verb-second, sentence bracket)
15. Basic conjunctions (und, oder, aber, denn)
16. Numbers, dates, time expressions
17. Plural formation (basic patterns)

### Hours of Study to Reach A1
- **Goethe-Institut estimate:** 60–150 hours of instruction
- **Typical intensive course:** 4–8 weeks (20 hours/week)
- **Typical standard course:** 12–16 weeks (4–6 hours/week)
- **Self-study (realistic):** 3–6 months depending on intensity

## 4. Goethe-Zertifikat A1: Start Deutsch 1 (Exam Format)

### Structure
| Section | Duration | Points |
|---------|----------|--------|
| Hören (Listening) | ~20 minutes | 25 points |
| Lesen (Reading) | 25 minutes | 25 points |
| Schreiben (Writing) | 20 minutes | 25 points |
| Sprechen (Speaking) | ~15 minutes (group) | 25 points |

**Pass mark:** 60% overall (60/100 points)

### Hören (Listening) – 3 parts
1. Short everyday dialogues – matching (6 items)
2. Short announcements/messages – true/false (4 items)
3. Short telephone messages – matching/multiple choice (5 items)

### Lesen (Reading) – 3 parts
1. Short everyday texts (ads, signs, notices) – matching (5 items)
2. Short informational texts – true/false (5 items)
3. Short advertisements – matching (5 items)

### Schreiben (Writing) – 2 parts
1. Fill in a form with personal information (5 items)
2. Write a short informal message/email (~30 words)

### Sprechen (Speaking) – 3 parts (usually in groups of 2–4)
1. Introduce yourself (name, age, country, languages, job, hobbies)
2. Ask for / give information based on word cards
3. Make a request / respond to a request based on word cards

## 5. Typical Course Pacing

### In a Standard Course (Volkshochschule / Language School)
- **Total duration for A1:** ~12–16 weeks
- **Pace:** ~1 chapter per week (with 4–6 hours instruction)
- **Including Plattform reviews:** add 1 week per Plattform (4 total)
- **Typical schedule:** 2 sessions × 2–3 hours per week

### For Self-Learners (Recommended Pace)
- **Comfortable pace:** 1.5–2 weeks per chapter
- **Total estimated time:** 18–28 weeks (4.5–7 months)
- **Daily study time:** 30–60 minutes
- **Weekly study time:** 5–7 hours

### Suggested Weekly Breakdown Per Chapter (self-study, ~10 days)
| Day | Activity |
|-----|----------|
| 1–2 | Kursbuch: Work through section A (new vocabulary, listening, reading) |
| 3 | Kursbuch: Section B + grammar focus |
| 4 | Arbeitsbuch: Exercises for sections A–B |
| 5–6 | Kursbuch: Section C + pronunciation |
| 7 | Arbeitsbuch: Exercises for section C + grammar review |
| 8 | Review: Vocabulary, grammar summary, additional practice |
| 9 | Speaking practice + listening review |
| 10 | Self-test / review before moving to next chapter |

## 6. How to Effectively Use Netzwerk Neu for Self-Study

### Using the Kursbuch (Textbook)
1. **Start each chapter** by looking at the chapter overview page (Lernziele)
2. **Work through sections in order** (A → B → C) – they build on each other
3. **Use the audio/video** via Klett Augmented app or allango platform – scan pages with your phone to access audio directly
4. **Don't skip listening exercises** – they develop comprehension and pronunciation
5. **Use the grammar boxes** as reference, but focus on communication first
6. **Do the mini-projects** even alone (write out what you would say)

### Using the Arbeitsbuch (Workbook)
1. **Complete after each Kursbuch section** (not all at once at chapter end)
2. **Use for reinforcement** – the exercises follow the same A/B/C structure
3. **Grammar exercises** are more systematic here than in the Kursbuch
4. **Writing exercises** provide structured practice for the exam
5. **Self-assessment** pages at the end of each workbook chapter help track progress
6. **Check answers** (solutions are available online or in a separate booklet)

### Using Online/Audio Materials
1. **Klett Augmented App / allango platform:**
   - Scan textbook pages to access audio clips directly
   - Video clips for each chapter (authentic situations)
   - Interactive exercises online
2. **Audio files:** Available for download from Klett website
3. **Online exercises:** Additional grammar and vocabulary drills
4. **Vocabulary trainer:** Digital flashcard system aligned with chapters

### Best Practices for Self-Learners
1. **Set a regular schedule** – consistency beats intensity
2. **Use spaced repetition** for vocabulary (Anki or similar alongside the book)
3. **Speak out loud** – repeat dialogues, record yourself, shadow the audio
4. **Review vocabulary lists** at the end of each chapter (Wortliste)
5. **Use the Plattform sections** as checkpoints – don't skip them
6. **Supplement with free resources** (see below) to get more listening/reading input
7. **Track progress** using can-do statements from the chapter overview
8. **Don't aim for perfection** – aim for understanding and basic production before moving on

## 8. Key Takeaways for Study Plan Design

1. **12 chapters** can be distributed across 18–24 weeks for comfortable self-study
2. **Each chapter = ~1.5 weeks** at 5–7 hours/week
3. **4 Plattform review sections** provide natural checkpoints (every 3 chapters)
4. **Grammar progression** is carefully sequenced – don't skip ahead
5. **Perfekt (past tense)** comes last (Kapitel 12) – plan extra time for this
6. **Exam practice** should be integrated from Plattform 2 onward
7. **Listening and speaking** need daily practice even in small doses
8. **Vocabulary review** is critical – use spaced repetition alongside the textbook
9. **DW Nicos Weg** maps well to Netzwerk Neu chapters for supplementary material
10. **The Arbeitsbuch is essential** for self-study – not optional
