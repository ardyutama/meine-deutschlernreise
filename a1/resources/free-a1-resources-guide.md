# Free Resources & Study Methods for German A1 Absolute Beginners

## 1. Podcasts & Audio Resources

### Coffee Break German
- **URL:** https://coffeebreaklanguages.com/coffeebreakgerman/
- **Type:** Audio podcast (15-20 min episodes)
- **Target skill:** Listening, speaking, grammar explanation
- **Cost:** Free podcast episodes; paid online course optional (£199/season)
- **Best for A1:** Season 1 (40 lessons) — starts from absolute zero, covers greetings, introductions, travel scenarios
- **How to use:** Listen to 1 episode per day during commute or walk. The teacher Mark learns alongside you, explaining how the language works rather than just drilling phrases.
- **Available on:** Apple Podcasts, Spotify, website
- **Access Season 1:** https://coffeebreaklanguages.com/tag/cbg-season-1/?order=asc

### GermanPod101
- **URL:** https://www.germanpod101.com/
- **Type:** Audio & video lessons (structured course)
- **Target skill:** Listening comprehension, vocabulary, pronunciation
- **Cost:** Free account (limited access to lesson library); paid plans for full access
- **Best for A1:** Absolute Beginner and Beginner pathways
- **How to use:** Sign up for free account. Use the "Absolute Beginner" series. Lessons break down conversations word-by-word with slowed-down audio. Good supplement for pronunciation practice.
- **Features:** Word lists, flashcards, line-by-line audio breakdowns, voice recording tools

### Slow German (mit Annik Rubens)
- **URL:** https://slowgerman.com/
- **Type:** Podcast — clear, slow German on cultural topics
- **Target skill:** Listening comprehension
- **Cost:** Free podcast; Premium membership for extra materials
- **Level:** B1+ (NOT suitable for absolute A1 beginners)
- **How to use:** Bookmark for later (around month 4-6 of study). Once you reach late A2/early B1, start listening to build comprehension of connected speech. Available on Spotify, Apple Podcasts, YouTube.
- **YouTube:** https://www.youtube.com/@slowgermanpodcast

### DW (Deutsche Welle) — Learn German
- **URL:** https://learngerman.dw.com/en/overview
- **Type:** Comprehensive free learning platform (video, audio, exercises)
- **Target skill:** All skills — listening, reading, grammar, vocabulary
- **Cost:** Completely free
- **Best A1 resources:**
  - **Nicos Weg** — Video course for beginners following Nico from Spain in Germany. Includes grammar pages and interactive exercises. https://learngerman.dw.com/en/nicos-weg/c-36519789
  - **Deutschtrainer** — 100 everyday topics with bilingual audio for vocabulary and pronunciation. https://learngerman.dw.com/en/deutschtrainer/c-56705009
  - **Langsam gesprochene Nachrichten** — Slowly-spoken news (better for B1+, save for later)
- **How to use:** Start with Nicos Weg — watch 1-2 episodes daily (5-10 min each). Complete the interactive exercises after each episode. Use Deutschtrainer for vocabulary building in parallel.

### Deutsch — warum nicht?
- **URL:** https://learngerman.dw.com/ (part of DW's offerings)
- **Type:** Classic audio course from Deutsche Welle
- **Target skill:** Listening, vocabulary, grammar (structured progression)
- **Cost:** Free
- **How to use:** Follow the story-based episodes sequentially. Good for learners who prefer narrative-based learning.

## 2. Free Apps That Complement Textbook Learning

### Anki (Spaced Repetition Flashcards)
- **URL:** https://apps.ankiweb.net/
- **Type:** Flashcard app with spaced repetition algorithm
- **Target skill:** Vocabulary retention, grammar patterns
- **Cost:** Free (Desktop, AnkiWeb, AnkiDroid); iOS app is paid
- **Best A1 decks to download (from AnkiWeb shared decks):**
  - "German A1 Vocabulary" — core 500-800 words
  - "Netzwerk Neu A1 Vocabulary" — if using this textbook
  - "German Sentences with Audio" — sentences in context
  - "German Gender: Der Die Das" — noun genders with mnemonics
- **How to use:**
  - Review cards every single day (15-20 min)
  - Add 10-15 new cards daily from your textbook chapter
  - Always include: German word, article (for nouns), example sentence, audio if possible
  - Create your own cards from Netzwerk Neu chapters for best retention

### Memrise
- **URL:** https://www.memrise.com/
- **Type:** Vocabulary app with video clips of native speakers
- **Target skill:** Vocabulary, pronunciation, listening
- **Cost:** Free tier available; Pro for full features
- **Best for A1:** Official German courses for beginners; community courses aligned with textbooks
- **How to use:** 10-15 minutes daily as vocabulary warm-up. Good for hearing real native pronunciation through video clips. Use as supplement to Anki, not replacement.

### Duolingo
- **URL:** https://www.duolingo.com/course/de/en/Learn-German
- **Type:** Gamified language app
- **Target skill:** Reading, writing, vocabulary, basic grammar
- **Cost:** Free (with ads); Plus for ad-free
- **How to use:** 
  - Use as a 5-10 min daily warm-up or cool-down, NOT as primary study tool
  - Good for keeping a streak/motivation on low-energy days
  - Complete 1-2 lessons per day maximum
  - Research shows it works for basics but lacks depth for grammar explanation
- **Limitations:** Doesn't explain grammar well; lacks speaking practice; sentences can be unnatural

## 3. Study Method Recommendations

### Optimal Daily Schedule (1-2 hours)

Based on polyglot and language coaching best practices:

#### Option A: 1 Hour Daily
| Time | Activity | Resource |
|------|----------|----------|
| 0-15 min | Anki review (spaced repetition) | Anki app |
| 15-35 min | Textbook study (new chapter content) | Netzwerk Neu A1 |
| 35-50 min | Listening practice | Coffee Break German OR Nicos Weg |
| 50-60 min | Active practice (write sentences, shadowing) | Notebook + audio |

#### Option B: 2 Hours Daily
| Time | Activity | Resource |
|------|----------|----------|
| 0-20 min | Anki review + add new cards from yesterday's study | Anki |
| 20-50 min | Textbook: new grammar + exercises | Netzwerk Neu A1 |
| 50-65 min | Grammar exercises online | Lingolia or Schubert-Verlag |
| 65-85 min | Listening (podcast or DW video) | Coffee Break German / Nicos Weg |
| 85-105 min | Active output (write 5 sentences, speak aloud) | Notebook, voice recorder |
| 105-120 min | Duolingo/Memrise (light review) | App |

### Spaced Repetition Best Practices for German

1. **Daily reviews are non-negotiable** — Even 10 minutes of Anki beats skipping a day
2. **New cards:** Add 10-15 new cards daily (no more! Overwhelm = quitting)
3. **Card format for German:**
   - Front: English word/phrase
   - Back: German word + article + example sentence + audio
   - For verbs: include conjugation (ich/du/er forms minimum)
4. **Gender system:** Always learn nouns WITH their article (der/die/das). Use color coding: blue=der, red=die, green=das
5. **Intervals:** Trust the algorithm. If you get it wrong, it resets — that's fine
6. **Sentence cards > individual words** after week 2-3

### How to Use Netzwerk Neu A1 Alongside Free Resources

**Weekly pattern (assuming 1 chapter per 1-2 weeks):**

| Day | Textbook | Free Resource Pairing |
|-----|----------|----------------------|
| Mon | New chapter: read dialogue, vocab list | Add new vocab to Anki; listen to related Coffee Break episode |
| Tue | Grammar explanation + textbook exercises | Lingolia: read same grammar topic + do free exercise |
| Wed | Workbook exercises (Arbeitsbuch) | Nicos Weg episode on same theme |
| Thu | Listening exercises from textbook | GermanPod101 or DW Deutschtrainer on same topic |
| Fri | Review chapter; textbook test yourself | Schubert-Verlag online exercises |
| Sat | Light review: Duolingo + Anki only | Catch up on missed Anki reviews |
| Sun | Active output: write short text using chapter vocab | Record yourself reading textbook dialogue aloud |

### Optimal Skill Balance for A1 Beginners

Based on language acquisition research and polyglot recommendations:

| Skill | % of Time | Why |
|-------|-----------|-----|
| **Listening** | 30% | Input hypothesis — comprehensible input is king at A1 |
| **Vocabulary** | 25% | You need ~1000 words for A1; SRS makes this efficient |
| **Grammar** | 20% | Enough to understand structure, not so much you can't speak |
| **Reading** | 15% | Reinforces vocabulary and grammar patterns |
| **Speaking/Writing** | 10% | Start small — read aloud, write sentences, build to conversations |

**Key principles from polyglots:**
- **Comprehensible input first** — Listen to things just slightly above your level
- **Active recall > passive review** — Test yourself, don't just re-read
- **Consistency > intensity** — 30 min every day beats 4 hours on Saturday
- **Use the language, don't just study it** — Even labeling objects at home in German counts
- **Grammar through patterns** — Notice patterns in input before studying rules

## 4. Free Grammar References & Exercises

### Grammar Reference Sites

#### Lingolia German Grammar
- **URL:** https://deutsch.lingolia.com/en/grammar
- **Type:** Comprehensive grammar reference with 1 free exercise per topic
- **Target skill:** Grammar understanding + practice
- **Topics covered:** Tenses, verbs, nouns/articles, pronouns, adjectives, declension, adverbs, prepositions, sentence structure
- **Special feature:** Verb conjugator — https://deutsch.lingolia.com/en/grammar/conjugator
- **How to use:** When your textbook introduces a grammar topic (e.g., Akkusativ), look up the same topic on Lingolia for a second explanation with different examples. Do the free exercise.

#### Schubert-Verlag Online Exercises
- **URL:** https://www.schubert-verlag.de/aufgaben/uebungen_a1/a1_uebungen_index.htm
- **Type:** Structured A1 grammar exercises (8 chapters, multiple exercises each)
- **Target skill:** Grammar application, listening, vocabulary
- **Cost:** Completely free
- **Topics by chapter:**
  - Kapitel 1: Sich vorstellen, Verben, Zahlen, Sprachen
  - Kapitel 2: Büro, Gegenteile, Verbformen, Präpositionen
  - Kapitel 3: Hotel, Artikel, Akkusativ, Wortschatz
  - Kapitel 4: Essen/Trinken, Personalpronomen, Präteritum (haben/sein)
  - Kapitel 5: Tagesablauf, trennbare Verben, Partizip II, Perfekt
  - Kapitel 6: Wetter, Reisen, Verkehrsmittel, Perfekt
  - Kapitel 7: Komparativ, Akkusativ/Dativ, Weg beschreiben
  - Kapitel 8: Dativ/Akkusativ, Körperteile, Modalverben
- **Includes:** Audio listening exercises (marked with 🎧 icon)
- **How to use:** Match these chapters roughly to your Netzwerk Neu progression. Do 2-3 exercises per study session as extra practice.

## 5. Putting It All Together — Week 1 Starter Plan

### Day 1: Setup
- [ ] Download Anki (desktop + phone)
- [ ] Download a "German A1 Core Vocabulary" deck
- [ ] Bookmark DW Nicos Weg and Schubert-Verlag
- [ ] Subscribe to Coffee Break German podcast
- [ ] Open Netzwerk Neu A1, Chapter 1

### Days 2-7: Build the Habit
- [ ] Morning (15 min): Anki reviews
- [ ] Study block (30-45 min): Netzwerk Neu chapter content
- [ ] Listening (15-20 min): 1 Coffee Break German Season 1 episode
- [ ] Evening (5-10 min): Duolingo for light practice OR add new Anki cards

### Key Rules
1. **Never skip Anki** — even 5 minutes counts
2. **One podcast episode per day** — don't binge, let it sink in
3. **Textbook first, apps second** — the textbook provides structure
4. **Speak from day 1** — even reading aloud to yourself counts
5. **Track your time** — aim for consistency, not perfection
